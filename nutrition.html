Macronutrients

Ever heard of the term Macronutrients?

Food is the most amaizing thing on this planet, offering us a wide varierty of beautiful flavours that sing to our pallets leaving us so spoilt that we never know when to say no. We just simply eat and eat and eat making it increasingly challenging to eat a clean and balanced diet (free of the following....deep fired food, high salt, sugar and fat content, processed, fast foods etc). A balanced diet offers our body the nutrients it needs to function at an optimum level so things like hormone production, muscle repair and nerve function, as well as many other important bodily functions.

So what exactly are Macronutrients?


We can easily identify macronutrients by the following, Proteins, Carbohydrates & Fats. These nutrients are crucial to the human body and without them the body will not be able to produce enough energy to function properly. Bodily functions include thinking, breathing, sneezing, urinating, plus many many more, and all of these functions require calories, and plenty of them.

Micronutrients

Minerals and Vitamins are mincronutrients are essential and play a very important role within the body, so it's important to ensure your'e consuming the recommended daily amount to help support the body to cleanse, regulate and rebuild itself. Let's speak about Minerals first because I don't hear enough people speaking about these little super hero's and their impactful benefits. In simple, the body is made up of 102 minerals, each mineral playing a significant role within the body. Minerals can be mainly found in fruit and vegetables which are a great way to not only source minerals but vitamins too. This is because plants are soil fed and soil is mineral rich. Minerals can also be found in fish (absorb minerals from their environments) and meat (absorb minerals from eating plants/grass) so there are plenty of ways to source minerals and include them into your diet. Now lets take a look at Vitamins and their role in helping the body to produce energy, repair cells and fight off disease. Vitamins are organic compounds which are essential for the body to function at a level that is optimum, there for it's important to include vitamins into your diet every day. There are two types of vitamins, these are fat (vitamins A, D, E & K) soluble and water soluble (vitamins B & C). Like Minerals, Vitamins can be sorced from vaious types of foods and can easily be ibtroduced into our daily diet. Its better to consume micronutrients through foods and herbal teas rather than taking suppliments. If you are going to head down the suppliments rout I woud highly recommend researching thr brand, the ingrients and the quality.
